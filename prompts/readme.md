## 📖 Available Prompts

### 1. 🧭 Socratic Questioning (The Clarity Compass)
**What it does:** Turns an AI into a thinking partner that asks insightful questions instead of giving you answers.

**Best for:**
- Untangling complex problems
- Challenging your assumptions
- Deep brainstorming sessions
- Academic research & thesis development

**Quick example:** Instead of "What's the best marketing strategy?" → The AI asks "What problem are you *really* trying to solve? Who does it hurt most if it's unsolved?"

👉 [Open full prompt →](./socratic-questioning-prompt.md)

---

### 2. ⚔️ Steel Man Argument (Red Team Analysis)
**What it does:** Strengthens your ideas by having an AI build the *strongest possible case against them*.

**Best for:**
- Stress-testing proposals before implementation
- Decision-making under uncertainty
- Identifying hidden vulnerabilities
- Academic debate preparation

**Quick example:** You propose "We should cut costs 20% this year." The AI responds with the strongest rational argument *against* it, grounded in evidence.

## 🎯 How to Use Any Prompt Here

1. **Copy the prompt text** from the file
2. **Paste into your favorite LLM** (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.)
3. **Add your topic/question** where it says `[User input here]`
4. **Hit enter** and follow the structured response

**Example workflow:**
```
[Open Socratic Questioning prompt]
Insert your problem: "I'm not sure if I should pivot my startup"
[Paste prompt + problem into Claude]
→ Get insightful questions back
→ Use them to think deeper
```

## 💭 Glossary: Prompt Techniques

| Technique | What It Does | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Socratic Questioning** | Asks questions instead of giving answers | You need to *think* harder, not get a quick answer |
| **Steel Man Argument** | Builds strongest case *against* your idea | Before big decisions, to find real flaws |
| **First Principles Thinking** | Breaks problems into fundamental truths | Stuck in conventional thinking; need fresh perspective |
| **Root Cause Analysis** | Asks "why?" repeatedly to find real cause | Something is broken; need to find *why* it's broken |
| **Pre-Mortem** | Imagines failure & works backward | Before launching, to anticipate problems |
